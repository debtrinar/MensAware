What is a Menstrual Cup?

A menstrual cup is a reusable, bell-shaped feminine hygiene product. It's made from flexible medical-grade silicone and is worn inside the vagina during menstruation to collect, rather than absorb, menstrual fluid. Unlike tampons or pads, a single cup can be reused for up to 10 years, making it an incredibly eco-friendly and cost-effective alternative.

How to Use Your Cup

1

Fold & Insert

Wash your hands. Find a comfortable position. Fold the cup (C-fold or punch-down) and gently insert it into your vagina, angled towards your tailbone.

2

Seal & Wear

Once inserted, the cup will pop open and create a light seal against the vaginal walls. You can run a finger around the base to ensure it's fully open. Wear for up to 12 hours.

3

Remove & Clean

Wash your hands. Gently pinch the base of the cup to break the seal, then slowly pull it out. Empty the contents, rinse with water, and reinsert. Sterilize between cycles.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I choose the right size? expand_more

Most brands offer two sizes. A smaller size is typically for those under 30 or who haven't given birth vaginally. A larger size is for those over 30, with a heavier flow, or who have given birth vaginally. Always check the brand's specific sizing guide.

Is it hard to insert and remove? expand_more

There is a learning curve, but it gets easier with practice. Relaxing your pelvic muscles is key. Experimenting with different folding techniques and positions (like squatting) will help you find what's most comfortable for your body.

Can I use a menstrual cup with an IUD? expand_more

Yes, but it's best to consult your doctor first. Ensure you break the seal by pinching the base of the cup before removal to avoid dislodging the IUD. Some users also choose to have their IUD strings trimmed shorter by their healthcare provider.

How do I clean my cup in a public restroom? expand_more

It's simpler than it sounds. You can either carry a small water bottle into the stall to rinse it, or just wipe it clean with toilet paper and reinsert. You can give it a more thorough wash with soap and water when you get home.
